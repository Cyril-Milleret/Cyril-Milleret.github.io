I am a quantitative ecologist. I especially like to apply statistical methods to make sense of data. 
At the moment, I am working halftime at the university of Norwegian life sciences ([NMBU](https://www.nmbu.no/en), Norway) and halftime at the [CEFE-CNRS](https://www.cefe.cnrs.fr/en/) at Montpellier (France)
where I am part of the [AEQG](https://www.nmbu.no/en/research/groups/applied-quantitative-ecology-group-aqeg) and [HAIR](https://human-animal-interactions.github.io/) group.
My work consists mostly in developing and applying statistical methods to estimate the demography and population size of wildlife populations.


# SCR

A large part of my time is spent in developing  spatial-capture recapture (SCR) models. 
These models are now used routinely to monitor wolf populations in [France](https://oliviergimenez.github.io/pubs/milleretetal2026.pdf) and in [Scandinavia](https://static02.nmbu.no/mina/publikasjoner/mina_fagrapport/pdf/mif103.pdf),
as well as [wolverines](https://static02.nmbu.no/mina/publikasjoner/mina_fagrapport/pdf/mif112.pdf) and [bears](https://static02.nmbu.no/mina/publikasjoner/mina_fagrapport/pdf/mif105.pdf) in Scandinavia. 

These models were also applied on the Pyrenean [bear](https://peercommunityjournal.org/articles/10.24072/pcjournal.646/), and Italian [wolverines](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37259636/) populations.
